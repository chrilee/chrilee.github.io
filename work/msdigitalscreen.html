Project:

Microsoft Digital Wall

Client | Microsoft

Role | UX / Visual Designer

Description

Microsoft asked Infusion to help build their digital screen to be displayed at the National Retail Federation in 2013.

Objective

Microsoft wanted a digital wall display in the middle of their booth at the National Retail Federation (NRF) in Washington. The digital screen was to be made with four screens side-by-side to create one large display. It needed to show the scheduling of the event, their Twitter feed, statistics around the booth and map of their partner booths. The wall also needed to blend and have the same look and feel with the rest of their booth, The project had to be designed and built within a monthâ€™s time.

Solution

For this project we had limited time to design and code the screens so I decided to split the design of the wall into four separate sections with each section being on an individual screen rather than have a design that spans several screens. This method is easier for the developer as each screen is independent from the next.

One important factor I considered when designing the wall was that I wanted it to look as close as possible to their current look for their booth. I followed one of their posters by using the same coloured tiles, similar feeling large images, and metro-like icons. The resulted product led patrons to be unaware that it was Infusion designed and built project because it blended in as part of the whole Microsoft experience.
