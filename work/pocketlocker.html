Project:

Pocket Locker Windows 8 Application

Client | Microsoft / St. Clements School

Role | UX / Visual Designer

Description

Network for Teaching Entrepreneurship (NFTE) program sponsored by Microsoft held a competition for schools to submit the most innovative idea, where the winning idea will have their project built. Six girls from St. Clements School won the competition with their Pocket Locker idea - a mobile organization and note-taking tool.

Objective

Microsoft asked Infusion to become the partner in bringing Pocket Locker to life. Infusion had to work with the students from St. Clements to design and build the product using the Windows 8 platform.

Solution

For this project we had a small team consisting of a developer, an art director, a project manager and myself as the designer and a short timeframe of two months to complete the application from start to finish. At the beginning of this project, we met with the client (students from St Clements) to gather what they wanted the application to do and figured out what the minimal viable product (MVP) was. Due to the fact that it was a Windows 8 application, I had to create the information architecture (IA) and wireframes that followed the Windows 8 paradigm. For the composites, I went with a design that was fun and representative of the locker concept, hence the attract screen looked like a locker. Also keeping in mind that the students like customization, they had the ability to change the locker photo and accent colour of their application. As for rest of the application, it was designed to look like the inside of a notebook with the look and feel of Windows 8.

The project was completed successfully and was well received by our clients.
