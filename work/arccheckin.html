Project:

Resort and Casino Check-in Application

Client | Infusion

Role | UX / Visual Designer

Description

Infusion hosted Sleepless, a technology-focused training program for a weekend where four teams of developers/designers are assigned projects that must be completed within an intense 26 hour timeframe and presented to a judging panel of senior executives and mock clients. The event enables employees to use the latest technologies, which are assigned on the day of. Each team has the right mix of required skillsets, including a designer, programmers, and a team lead, and is assigned a unique client.

Objective

The objective of this bootcamp is to create a check-in application for a casino and resort to quickly and efficiently identify guests and sign them in without having to do it at the front desk. On top of checking in the guests, the applucation should also provide loyalty statistics and possible upgrade options.

Solution

My team consisted of a team lead, technical lead, two developers and myself as the UX and visual designer. The technology assigned to us was PhoneGap, which we had to use to deploy on three tablet devices: Android tablet, iPad and Microsoft Surface.

Due to the limited time and the fact that we are using PhoneGap, I decided to create design that has a generic interface that can be used on all three platforms. My focus for the design is create something that looks modern and elegant that fits with the look of a high-end casino and thus I used a lot of beautiful imagery for the background while still focusing on the foreground interface.

My job during the 26 hour timeframe was to create wireframes and composites to show the client every 3 hours as well as creating assets for the developers to implement.

In the end, we were able to deploy our application on iPad and Android.
